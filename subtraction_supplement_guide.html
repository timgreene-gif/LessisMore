THE SUBTRACTION SUPPLEMENT GUIDE

Stop Taking. Start Removing.

The supplement industry sells you back what the food industry took away. But you cannot supplement your way out of a bad diet and lifestyle. This guide maps what depletes each nutrient so you can remove the offender instead of adding another pill.

DISCLAIMER: Educational purposes only. Not medical advice. Do not stop prescribed medications or supplements without consulting your healthcare provider.

The Repeat Offenders

The same substances deplete multiple nutrients. Remove these and you address many deficiencies at once:

OffenderWhat It Depletes
SugarMagnesium, B Vitamins, Vitamin C, Vitamin D, Calcium, Chromium
AlcoholB Vitamins, Magnesium, Zinc, Vitamin C, Vitamin D, Iron, Calcium
Excess CaffeineIron, Calcium, Vitamin D, Magnesium, B Vitamins

THE LEVERAGE PLAY: Remove sugar alone and you address deficiencies in Magnesium, B Vitamins, Vitamin C, Vitamin D, Calcium, and Chromium. That's 6+ supplements replaced by ONE subtraction.

Part 1: The Science

Magnesium

Involved in 300+ enzymatic reactions. The most commonly supplemented mineral.

RemoveWhy It Depletes
SugarSignificant magnesium required to metabolize sugar. Low Mg impairs blood sugar regulation, which depletes more Mg‚Äîa vicious cycle.
AlcoholIncreases urinary excretion. Up to 50% of heavy drinkers are deficient.
Excess CaffeineHigh doses increase urinary excretion by reducing renal reabsorption.
Chronic StressMg needs can DOUBLE under stress. Low Mg increases stress susceptibility‚Äîanother vicious cycle.
High-dose Vit DBody requires Mg to metabolize Vitamin D. Excessive supplementation depletes Mg stores.

Signs: Muscle cramps, eye twitches, anxiety, insomnia, fatigue, headaches. Foods: Pumpkin seeds, spinach, dark chocolate (85%+), almonds, avocado.

B Vitamins

Essential for energy, mood, and nervous system. Water-soluble‚Äîmust be replenished daily.

RemoveWhy It Depletes
Sugar / Refined CarbsB1, B2, B3, B5 required to metabolize glucose. Refined carbs provide calories without the B vitamins needed to process them.
AlcoholLeading cause of B1 deficiency in developed countries. Up to 80% of heavy drinkers deficient in folate.
Chronic StressBurned rapidly for neurotransmitter production (serotonin, GABA, dopamine).
Birth ControlDepletes B6, B12, and folate with long-term use.

Signs: Fatigue, brain fog, depression, numbness/tingling, mouth sores. Foods: Eggs, liver, nutritional yeast, salmon, leafy greens.

Vitamin C

Critical for immunity, antioxidant protection, collagen, and iron absorption.

RemoveWhy It Depletes
SugarGlucose and Vitamin C use the SAME transporter protein‚Äîand it prefers glucose. Sugar literally blocks Vitamin C absorption.
Chronic StressAdrenal glands require Vitamin C to produce cortisol and adrenaline. High stress = rapid depletion.
SmokingEach cigarette depletes ~25mg. Smokers need 35mg/day more than non-smokers.
AlcoholReduces absorption and increases excretion.

Signs: Slow wound healing, easy bruising, bleeding gums, frequent infections. Foods: Bell peppers, citrus, strawberries, kiwi, broccoli.

Vitamin D

Essential for immunity, bone health, mood. Functions more like a hormone than a vitamin.

RemoveWhy It Depletes
Fructose / SugarIncreases enzyme that DEGRADES Vitamin D while decreasing the enzyme that synthesizes it.
Indoor LifestylePrimary source is sunlight. 15-20 min of midday sun beats supplementation.
Chronic StressExcess cortisol blocks Vitamin D receptors, preventing absorption even when levels are adequate.
ObesityVitamin D is fat-soluble and gets sequestered in fat tissue, reducing bioavailability.

Signs: Fatigue, bone pain, muscle weakness, depression, frequent illness. Foods: Fatty fish, egg yolks, UV-exposed mushrooms. Sun remains primary source.

Iron

Critical for energy and oxygen transport. Most common deficiency in women, vegetarians, and athletes.

RemoveWhy It Depletes
Coffee/Tea with MealsReduces absorption up to 80%. Polyphenols bind to non-heme iron. Separate by at least 1 hour.
Chronic StressDepletes iron stores‚Äîespecially problematic for women and vegetarians.
Antacids / PPIsStomach acid required to convert iron to absorbable form.
Excess CalciumCalcium competes with iron for absorption. Take at different times.

Signs: Fatigue, pale skin, shortness of breath, cold hands/feet, restless legs. Foods: Red meat, liver, oysters, sardines, spinach, lentils. Pair plant sources with Vitamin C.

Zinc

Essential for immunity, wound healing, taste/smell, testosterone, and 300+ enzyme functions.

RemoveWhy It Depletes
AlcoholSignificantly lower zinc in alcohol-dependent individuals. Affects both absorption and excretion.
Chronic StressDepletes zinc, which then increases inflammation and illness risk.
High-Phytate DietGrains and legumes contain phytic acid that binds zinc. Solution: soaking, sprouting, or fermenting.
Vegetarian DietPlant zinc less bioavailable. May need 50% more than omnivores.

Signs: Frequent infections, slow healing, loss of taste/smell, hair loss, low libido. Foods: Oysters, beef, crab, pumpkin seeds, chickpeas, cheese.

Calcium

Critical for bones, muscles, nerves, blood clotting. Works with magnesium, vitamin D, and K2.

RemoveWhy It Depletes
SugarHigh sugar/insulin increases calcium excretion through urine.
Excess CaffeineEach cup of coffee causes ~5mg calcium loss. Accumulates with heavy use.
High SodiumFor every 2,300mg sodium, you lose ~40mg calcium.
Sedentary LifestyleWeight-bearing exercise signals bones to retain calcium.

Signs: Muscle cramps, numbness, brittle nails, easy fractures. Foods: Dairy, sardines (with bones), leafy greens, almonds.

Chromium

Essential for insulin function and blood sugar regulation. Heavily depleted by modern diet.

RemoveWhy It Depletes
Sugar / Refined CarbsBody uses chromium to process sugar. High intake = depletion. Creates insulin resistance cycle.
Processed FoodsRefining strips chromium. White flour has 98% less than whole wheat.
Stress / ExerciseBoth increase urinary chromium excretion.

Signs: Blood sugar swings, sugar cravings, fatigue after eating. Foods: Broccoli, potatoes, garlic, beef, whole grains.

Potassium

Critical for heart, muscles, nerves, blood pressure. Most people get less than half the recommended amount.

RemoveWhy It Depletes
Alcohol / CaffeineDiuretic effect increases potassium excretion.
High SodiumSodium and potassium work in opposition. High sodium = potassium loss.
DiureticsMany blood pressure medications deplete potassium significantly.

Signs: Muscle weakness/cramps, fatigue, constipation, palpitations. Foods: Potatoes, bananas, avocados, spinach, salmon, coconut water.

Omega-3 Fatty Acids

Not a depletion story‚Äîan imbalance story. Modern diets are extremely skewed toward omega-6, creating systemic inflammation.

ImbalanceWhy It Matters
Seed Oils (excess)Soybean, corn, canola high in omega-6. Ideal ratio is 2:1 or lower. Most Americans at 15:1+.
Processed FoodsNearly all contain seed oils, dramatically skewing the ratio.
Low Fish IntakeEPA/DHA primarily from fatty fish. Plant omega-3s (ALA) convert poorly.

Signs: Dry skin, brittle hair, poor concentration, joint pain, mood issues. Foods: Salmon, mackerel, sardines, fish oil, walnuts, flaxseed.

Part 2: Implementation

The Priority Stack

Do not change everything at once. Work in order:

1SugarHighest leverage‚Äîaddresses 6+ deficiencies. Start here.

Eating Out Strategies

Any restaurant: Protein + vegetables as base. Dressings on side. Skip bread basket. "Grilled" over "crispy."

Fast food: Burger no bun, grilled not breaded, side salad not fries, water not soda.

Social situations: Eat before events. "No thanks, I'm good"‚Äîno explanation needed.

Hidden Sugar Names

Obvious: Sugar, brown sugar, cane sugar, coconut sugar, powdered sugar.

Syrups: High-fructose corn syrup, corn syrup, maple syrup, agave, rice syrup, malt syrup.

The "-ose" family: Glucose, fructose, dextrose, sucrose, maltose.

Sneaky: Maltodextrin, fruit juice concentrate, evaporated cane juice, barley malt, molasses.

Rule: If any of these are in the first 3 ingredients, reconsider.

Troubleshooting

"I caved and ate sugar." One meal doesn't undo progress. Next meal, back to baseline. What triggered it? Address the root.

"Cravings are unbearable." Eat more protein and fat‚Äîyou may be under-eating. Are you sleeping enough?

"No time to cook." Sunday prep: 2 hours = 5 days of lunches. Rotisserie + salad = 5-min dinner.

"I feel worse, not better." First 2 weeks can feel worse (withdrawal). Past week 3: eating enough? electrolytes?

"Social pressure." Prepare your line: "No thanks, I'm good." No explanation needed.

Minimum Effective Dose

Not ready to go all-in? These 4 changes give you 80% of the benefit:

1. Cut added sugar by 50% ‚Äî If sugar is in first 3 ingredients, skip it.

2. No caffeine with meals ‚Äî 1-hour buffer. Protects mineral absorption. Free and easy.

3. Sleep 7+ hours ‚Äî Non-negotiable for stress recovery and nutrient protection.

4. Move 20 minutes daily ‚Äî Walk counts. Improves everything.

Personalization Notes

Women: Higher iron needs during menstruating years. Ca/D critical at menopause. Birth control depletes B vitamins, Mg, Zn. Pregnancy increases folate, iron, calcium, omega-3 needs.

Men: Zinc critical for testosterone‚Äîalcohol especially problematic. Iron overload more common than deficiency (test first).

Over 50: B12 absorption decreases (may need sublingual). Vitamin D production from sun decreases. Stomach acid decreases.

Athletes: Iron, Mg, K, Zn, B vitamins all used/lost more rapidly. May need above RDA.

Plant-based: B12 supplementation essential. Iron less bioavailable (pair with C). Zinc needs 50% higher. Consider algae-based EPA/DHA.

Honest Exceptions

Some supplementation may still be warranted:

  • Vitamin D ‚Äî If far from equator, work indoors, or have dark skin
  • B12 ‚Äî If plant-based or over 50
  • Omega-3s ‚Äî If you don't eat fatty fish 2-3x per week
  • Magnesium ‚Äî If chronic stress, heavy exercise, or poor sleep
  • Iron ‚Äî If menstruating, pregnant, or confirmed deficiency (test first)

These should be exceptions, not the rule. Address root causes first.
